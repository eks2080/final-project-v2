How do three grocery chains serve New Yorkers?

The Food Retail Expansion to Support Health (FRESH) program is a New York City initative to increase fresh food accecss in under served neighborhoods. Established in 2009, the program provides tax benefits to grocery store operators and developers who build or renovate spaces to grocery stores. FRESH eligible areas span all five boroughs and 28 projects have been approved through the program to date.

Three nationwide grocery store chains - Whole Foods, Trader Joes, and Aldi - are available throughout New York City. To understand the gaps in grocery access through the FRESH program, this map shows the locations of these stores across the city. Readers can also toggle the median-income of each area to gain a better understanding of where these stores are and are not.
