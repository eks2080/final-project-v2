Grocery Access and Household Income in New York City

The Food Retail Expansion to Support Health (FRESH) program is a New York City initative to increase fresh food accecss in underserved neighborhoods, providing tax and/or density incentives for developers to design and rent out retail space to grocery stores in new or renovated buildings.

This map explores how FRESH zoning eligibility compares to median household income in New York City neighborhoods.
